Connections between personal interests and professional experiences.

Throughout the entirety of my life, I have found that there can be an overlap between the two sides of every person. The one we present at the office, and the one we present to those closer to us, and can find success in doing so without crossing the line into impropriety. They can serve as learning experiences to undertand people, to interact with others, how to undertand and empathize with them. The experiences that one has in their personal life can end up being an unexpected benefit to their career. They just need to be applied properly. This is especially true for librarians who may be expected to interact with, and aid, people from all walks of life.

Understanding the connections, and making use of them.

Not everything can be transferred over from personal life to day to day operations of a library, but the lessons we learn from them can be very useful. For this we need to pick out the experiences on an individual basis that can best inform us for situations in the future. But by applying lessons learned from various hobbies, I feel that I can bring the same passion to my duties as I have experienced during leisure time. As many people have said, so long as you enjoy what you do, you will never have to work a day in your life.

Personal hobbies which can tie into librarianship:

  • Tabletop Roleplaying experience and book keeping.
  • Ochestral Experience.
  • Volunteer efforts with other libraries (not always directly working with the library staff).
  • Casual interest in world history, mythology, and cultures.

Benefits of these personal hobbies on my professional experiences:

  • The small team building efforts of Tabletop Roleplaying Games helps a person to better work in a group for the overall benefit. Even though rolling dice and telling stories together is now the same as managing a library and dealing with patrons. But it does allow for a level of experience when it comes to dynamically reacting to unforseen circumstances. It helps a person to be quick on their feet, and can help one to come to embrace newcomers with a sense of comraderie. It also brings with it the added benefit of gaining a better understanding of the current cultural landscape, to understand what the younger generation of patrons are interested in and being able to meet them at their level. This means that the library is better able to bridge the generational divide and help patrons associate libraries with a positive experience.
  • By having played in an orchestra, and understanding musical theory, it provided me with a way to understand how important it is to be part of a team and contribute as much as I can without overshadowing others. Everyone working together in harmony allows a song to truly be great, and this idea can apply to any workplace, not just libraries. The very ideal of cooperation is what matters, but understanding it in practice is just as important.
  • My volunteer efforts have given me a wide range of experiences in how academic and public libraries operate, and how different they can be. I feel that this has allowed me to better appreciate the library ecosystem in a few of its forms. The nuances of each individual library have to be taken on a case-by-case basis, and I feel I have a better grasp on that after volunteering for different libraries.
  • MY interest in the various different topics of the world, and what makes people who they are, works to provide me with an understanding of the human experience. There is no one specific way to truly treat o interact with a nother person. But by understaning the wider diaspora of humanity, it helps to cut down on the chances of a cultural faux pas. It also brings with it the added benefit of how best to empathize with others and meet them halfway. We must always understand, and recognize, that everyone is an individual, and yet also part of a greater whole that fills in their identity. I also feel that this wide ranging field of knowledge would help me to better provide for the needs of patrons who come seeking books on just about any subject realted To the human experience from across the globe.
